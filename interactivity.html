    Beep Boop.

    When you think of interactivity, I know the first thing that comes to mind is this overall-wearing plumber. He's iconic and represents how highly interactive and responsive video games can be. But interactivity goes way beyond video games.

    1. Museum Displays
    2. Social Media
    3. ChatBots

    All of these things are completely operated by their user or user(s). That's pretty powerful when it comes to getting people hooked.

    Personally, I was not a TV or movie watcher growing up. Sure, I watched kids shows like Phineas and Ferb, but I never really watched mainstream shows on Netflix and Max.

    I think this is because the interactivity of the internet growing up. I was an early YouTube kid, watching The Yogscast and AntVenom before Minecraft was "cool". I loved how tied in they were to their communities. Obviously there's a parasocial aspect to that, but I didn't really pay it much mind as a 9 year old.
